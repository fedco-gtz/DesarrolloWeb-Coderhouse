PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION

What is Personal Data?

Personal Data is all personal information referring to natural persons or persons of a certain ideal existence or determinable.

What law protects Personal Data and what is its objective?

The law that protects Personal Data is No. 25,326. The main objective of this law and its complementary regulations, is the comprehensive protection of personal data recorded in files, registries, data banks or other technical data processing mechanisms, whether these data public or private, granting protection to people regarding their right to honor and privacy, as well as as well as guarantee access to the information that is recorded about them, in accordance with the established in article 43, third paragraph of the National Constitution.

How does Happy Pets USA put these regulations into practice?

In compliance with the provisions of Art. 6 of Law 25,326, when we collect personal data from the individuals, we previously inform their owners of the purpose for which said data will be processed and who your recipients or class of recipients may be. Likewise, we will inform the owner of the data the existence of the file, registry, data bank, electronic or of any other type, of which in question and the identity and address of the person responsible and we will define the mandatory or optional nature of the answers to the proposed questionnaire, especially when sensitive data is involved. In addition, we will inform you of the consequences of providing the data, of the refusal to to do so or their inaccuracy. We will also notify the data owner of the possibility to exercise your rights, which consists of access, rectification and deletion of data.

In By virtue of the above, Happy Pets informs that the aforementioned rights of the data owner They include the possibility of requesting information from the control body regarding the existence of files, records, bases or personal data banks, their purposes and the identity of their responsible for their person. In this sense, the owner of the data, after accreditation of his identity has the right to request and obtain information about your personal data included in the public or private data banks intended to provide reports. Those obliged to inform must do it in a clear way, free of codifications and, where appropriate, accompanied by an explanation, in language accessible to the average knowledge of the population, of the terms used. Broadly speaking, all person has the right to have them rectified, updated and, when appropriate, deleted or subject to confidentiality the personal data of which you are the owner, which are included in a data bank.

The owner of the personal data has the power to exercise the right to access to them free of charge at intervals of no less than six (6) months, unless proven a legitimate interest for this purpose in accordance with the provisions of article 14, paragraph 3 of Law No. 25,326.
